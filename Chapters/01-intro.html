Introduction and Basic Concepts

Units

SI base units

length m
mass kg
time s
temp K

SI derived units

area m^2
volume m^3
density kg/m^3
force N = kg*m/s^2
energy J = N*m = kg*m^2/s^2
pressure Pa = N/m^2 = kg/m*s^2

Properties of a System

System: a quantity of matter or region of space under study

A system can be Closed (control mass) or Open (control volume)
There are two types of properties:
Extensive and Intensive
Extensive: proportional to the size of the system - mass, volume
Intensive: independent of the size of the system - temperature, pressure

States, Processes and Cycles

System can be in many states

The State Postulate

For a pure substance in one phase, the state is specified by any two intensive properties.
For a pure substance in two phase equilibrium, the sate is specified by any one intensive property.

Process

Going from one state to the other

Cycle

A process that begins and ends at the same state.

Temperature Scales

Kelvin is absolute
